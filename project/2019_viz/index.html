Information Aesthetics

Role: Visual Designer

Contribution: Concept, Design, Build | 4 Month

Outcome: 40-inch square posters

Overview

I’m taking the class of “Data Visualization and Information Aesthetics” at Parsons School of Design. In this class, I will create a total of four posters and two interactive visualization projects.

In the first half of the semester, I created three posters for specific topics such as Man-made product, Nature and Society. I learned the basic techniques of data visualization in a series of design processes from ideation, source data discovery to iterative prototyping.

In the second half of the semester, based on the knowledge of the first half, I will craft one more poster and two interactive projects using d3.js and other javascript libraries.

#1: Man-made Product

I picked up the traditional Japanese costume called Junihitoe to introduce Japanese culture, and expressed characteristic information such as being 12 layers of kimono and being very heavy.

Using the grid system, I arranged the information so that the viewer’s eyes flow naturally from the image of the impressive kimono in the center to the surrounding information.

#2: Nature

I picked up the solar eclipse forecast because I was interested in astronomy and wanted to draw a planetary arc using the earth as a canvas. I expressed the mechanism of the eclipse and where the total eclipse, annular eclipse and hybrid eclipse will occur from 2019 to 2031.

I was struggled how to arrange information on location and date information of eclipse. In the iteration 1, I tried the idea of displaying the date information on the location of the eclipse. However, after receiving the feedback that the information is complicated, I decided to display the information of the place and the information of the date separately in the iteration 2.

#3: Society

I wanted to compare countries in the world in the field of information, so I found the worldwide investigation data of mobile data consumption per SIM per Month in 2018. I draw the ranking of mobile data usage all over the world.

I wanted to experiment a new method, so I tried the 2.5D expression. While continuing trial and error, I came up with the idea of using the binary number as a bar chart, and finally expressed the amount of data of each country in binary representation such as 14Gigabytes is represented as 1101000010011101110000110000000000 in binary.
