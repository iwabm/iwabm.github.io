UBER UX RESEARCH AND DESIGN

This was an academic project and we worked with Uber. Our team proposed the new function of Uber that makes it easy for drivers and riders to meet. From perspectives of both drivers and riders, we created an user journey map and wireframes.

Oct, 2018
SVA IXD Summer

Background

Uber wanted to make cities more accessible

This was an academic project of Summer Intensive course of User Research Methods in SVA, US. We worked with a director of Uber and interviewed with him in the beginning. We understood one of the main focus of Uber was how drivers and riders find each other and ensure their identity.

Problem

How might we ensure drivers and riders to find each other and confirm their identity perfectly?

As a field work, our team went to the JFK airport and used Uber. We had an experience that we couldn’t specify the driver easily because there were a lot of cars in the airport terminal and also other people were using the ride-sharing App. So one car stopped in front of us, but that was another person’s car. This moment was upset and so frustrating.

Concept

A driver and a rider can communicate about detailed location regardless of their language

In this situation, our team proposed the new function of Uber that makes it easy for drivers and riders to meet.

Design Process

Uber Experience Design based on Empathzing

Within 5 classes in 5 weeks, we made a field research, problem definition, ideation and prototyping and critique. We formed the small team and made a solution together as a UX/UI practitioner.

Method

Install Methodology of UX Design

Empathize

How did we know the right problem?

As a field research, we went to the JFK and used Uber App. Using AEIOU Guide, we recorded all interactions and experiences we had, and also interviewed with a real driver how to do his work usually.

In the interview, we confirmed that he had a problem to pick up a rider. Our driver told that he had a mistake that he picked up the wrong person who has a same first name. Also, he had a language problem because sometimes people who cannot speak English ride especially in the airport. We understood how difficult to pick up a right person perfectly.

AEIOU Guide and Debrief Insight

Define

Define Persona & As-Is Context

After the research, we made personas and summarize a current context to confirm and our understanding.

Ideate

Brainstorm and Decide Ideas

We conducted the brainstorming. One of the bold idea was issuing the 4-digit code for driver and rider and confirm it. Another idea was set the uber official meeting spot.

After the ideation, we selected the idea in terms of perfecting the meeting and prioritized 2 main ideas. One was automatic translation, and the other was realtime chat which wasn’t released in 2015.

According to this core idea, we made an user journey map and wireframes.

Prototype

Customer Journey Map and Wireframes

Things we tried, things that did not work

When we reviewed our first wireframe internally, we found we should consider the function for next use. Our idea provides the function to text a detailed location to the driver, but in the next time, it is frustrating to text the same location once again. So we added a function to save the text you write for the next use.

Evaluate

Final Presentation

Finally, our concept was evaluated by the final presentation of the class. Our professor said that our concept that promotes communication and translation make sense. However, our wireframe needed to think about another form of input such as voice or pictures because text communication still takes time for users. We needed more iteration to be the interaction natural for the user.

How did we measure success?

I think the project should be measured by quantitative and qualitative standard. In terms of quantitative view, we can measure the actual time until drivers and riders finally meet in the airport again. On the other hand, as a qualitative method, we also need to ask inquiries with drivers and riders to know how much their frustration is solved.

Learning

What is the tradeoffs?

Depth of communication vs time taking. Driver and rider must communicate about a detailed location. But if the number of communications increases, it takes time and workload for drivers and riders is also increased. We should not be like a messaging app, but we need to communicate the detail.

What did we learn?

I think user interview needs not just a talking skill but a well-prepared strategy. To have a good preparation enables us to extract a good insight from user.

In this class, the professor told us these strategies of interview. For example, we should go to interview with at least 3 people and devided the role separately. One focus on the interview, another one focus on the minutes, and the final one focus on recording. It means interviewing is not only just a talking, but also a strategic activity. Another strategy is to prepare questions beforehand. To think about why we ask this question and what we want to extract is the most important part of the interview. Without these thinkings, our interview will be lost the way.
