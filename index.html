About Me

Masaki is an award-winning research-oriented interaction designer experienced in a wide array of projects from tangible products to intangible services.

Currently, he is in an MFA Design + Technology program at Parsons School of Design to refine UX research methodologies and explore social and planetary scale problem-solving such as speculative design and trans-disciplinary design beyond user-centered design.

Originally he is from Tokyo, Japan. He grew up with anime, game, and PC, they made him proceed to Computer Science major at the University of Tokyo. He researched the interactive transparent display in the HCI context. HCI is interdisciplinary field and he needed not only coding but also design, psychology, and human factors. Thus he started to be interested in Design and previously worked at IBM Design for 10 years as a UX/UI designer to bridge human and technology. Masaki’s expertise is finding the right questions to solve and do iterative prototyping to build great products. He is the most excited in the moment of turning research insights into valuable functions with intimate collaboration. Because Masaki believes we cannot come up with an innovative solution without a great question.

However, in his experience, he thought there were some limitations of user-centered design because the user-centered approach focused on the current context and aimed to solve the immediate problems of users. Thus it seemed a little bit difficult to create more visionary or disruptive products. That’s why Masaki came back to school to explore beyond user-centered Design. He has an eye of both the user-focus and critical mind, and loves swimming in between academic and practical field.

Outside of work, He love traveling and shooting pictures. He got some photography awards in Japan and joined a group exhibition in NY.
