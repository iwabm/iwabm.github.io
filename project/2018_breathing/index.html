Role: Critical Designer

Outcome: Physical Prototype (submitting a paper for CHI)

Project Overview

I’m submitting this project to CHI2019 Late Breaking Work.

We present a Breathing Chair that changes its state and shape to prevent current sedentary lifestyle and help in shaping a healthy living habit. The Breathing chair tracks how long people have been sitting, and its surface swells physically like a mammal’s lung when people sit in the Breathing Chair for a long time. In so doing, it nudges people to stand up gently to avoid health risks of prolonged sitting. After a while, the Breathing chair quietly shrinks to its original state organically. This state-changing aspect of the Breathing Chair is almost physical reminder for people to stand up and leads them to shape a healthy lifestyle. In this paper, we describe the concept, design, and implementation method of the Breathing Chair. We also conducted the preliminary qualitative study amongst a sample of seven users and demonstrated the nudging aspect of Breathing Chair. Finally, we presented how it can be used for future applications.

Problem

Our body is designed for regular movement, but most people spend the bulk of their day sitting still instead. Especially, knowledge workers sit at their desks during the day, commute by car, and relax on the couch watching TV or on their computers in the evenings.

An independent survey that Ergotron commissioned [1] found that nearly 70 percent of full-time American workers hate sitting, yet 86 percent are required to do it all day, every day. As a result, they are sitting on the chair for an average of 13 hours on weekdays. According to a study of weekday total sitting hours in 20 countries around the world, the median daily total sitting time for 20 countries was 300 minutes. Also, Japan and Saudi Arabia were the longest in 420 minutes and 20 countries [2].

Research results that the health risk is increased by sitting too much has been reported worldwide, and attention is rising. As a representative study on the risk of death, there are investigations in Australia [3] that tracked approximately 220,000 people over the age of 45 years for three years, compared to those with a sitting time of fewer than 4 hours per day over 8 hours 1.15 times, people with more than 11 hours have a 1.4 times higher risk of total mortality. According to the latest study, it has been pointed out that the sedentary life is also related to the function of the brain [4], and it is required that practical interventions aimed at reducing and replacing sedentary behavior with intermittent light intensity physical activity to help slow cognitive decline.

Related Work

There are two major approaches to tackle this problem. One is an approach from ergonomics. Furniture makers have been developing an ergonomic chair not to get tired even if it sits for a long time [5]. However, the latest studies show that it is more important to reduce the time to sit and increase the time to move. Deborah Rohm Young’s group [6] suggests that prolonged sitting is harmful to health because sedentary behaviors usually have an little energy expenditure of 1.5 metabolic equivalents (METs) or less while slow, leisurely walking or light housework uses around 2.5 METs, and vigorous to moderate physical activity uses around 3 METs or more.

The other approach is from user experience design to increase the standing time of people. Height adjustable table [7] enables workers to work while standing. Buckley’s group reports that standing-based office work encourages signs of attenuating post-prandial glycaemic excursion [8]. However, Baker’s research group assessed that prolonged standing might also have health and productivity impacts [9]. Data that it is not good for your health whether you are sitting too hard or too standing has been reported.

Apple watch [10] provides another solution. It detects periods of no move of the person wearing it and urges him/her to stand up and walk. It can announce to the user at an appropriate timing for walking based on the biometric data, but the user may not perform the actual operation or accept the advice in some cases. In summary, based on the human-centered design, these products consider how to provide comfort and a healthy posture on the premise of human physical characteristics and limitations.

Concept

Breathing Chair offers the novel way to prevent prolonged sitting by chair breathing. When people sit for a long time, the chair breathes organically and deform into a difficult shape to sit. It leads people toward a state of standing up in a natural way. After a while, the chair quietly returns to its original state. Our approach is from ontological design, in which objects shape human beings, unlike conventional human-centered product designs mentioned in previous studies. Escobar argues “We design tools, and these tools design us back” in his book of ontological design [11]. We synthesized with and created Breathing Chair based on this nondualism concept that our individuals, materials and the world are all united and that there is no center.

Discussion of Shape

In addition to the breathing chair, we explored some shapes of the optimal deforming chair.

A heating chair that makes users stand up as the temperature gets hot.

A folding chair that makes users stands up by changing the shape to a vertical shape.

An animal chair that changes into the shape of an animal and gives a more organic impression.

After the ideation, we organized these four ideas on two axes.

One axis we considered was mechanical vs. organic. Mechanical way is functional for users. However, this chair makes people sudden change, and it is not our intention to let the user feel “being forced to a machine.” Sudden mechanical change seems ruthless and irritates users. Therefore, we judged that the organic way is more appropriate in this project and wanted users to think “the chair is also a living thing and so it cannot be helped.”

The other axis is state-changing vs. shape-changing. Chairs in the upper row change state like a cold state and a hot state, a shrinking state and a swelling state, whereas chairs in the lower row are only deforming from a chair to a different shape. We judged that the state-changing way is more appropriate because this way gives users more impression of living things.

As a result of evaluating with the above two axes, we finally chose the idea of Breathing Chair.

Implementation

Regarding implementation, it consists of two functions. One is measuring the time sitting. The force sensor on the seating surface measures the time since a person sits on the chair. The other is controlling breathing. In this prototype, we use the balloon, and it stretched when people were seated for a certain period.

According to Patel et al.’s research [12], it is important to walk up and walk around many times a day. Also, if you stand for two minutes every hour, lipid, blood sugar, and blood pressure will improve. Therefore, we adjusted the balloon to swell for about 2 minutes when the user continues sitting for 1 hour. The state of actual operation is shown in the following image. We confirmed that the bulging of the balloon pushes the user forward.

Conclusion and Future Work

In this paper, we propose the Breathing Chair which changes its shape to make people stand up to prevent prolonged sitting. When people sit for 1 hour, the chair breathes organically and deform into a difficult shape to sit. After 2 minutes, the chair quietly returns to its original state. It leads people toward a state of standing up in a natural way. We introduced the concept, design and running prototype.

As for a future iteration, there are two considerations to improve the quality of the product. One is the form itself. In this prototype, we were able to confirm that the breathing chair creates the force to make people stand up, so we would like to consider more organic shape such as a breathable fabric instead of a balloon. The other is the configuration of parameters. It is better to adjust the interval according to the usage environment, the age of the user, the use case, etc. Regarding a future application, we would like to install this product to schools and offices and to lead sedentary people to healthy lifestyles by an organic breathing chair.
