LimpiDual Touch

2009, Interactive Media
Academic Work
Designed and proposed interactive limpid display with dual-sided touch sensing.

concept: Users can interact from the rear side of the display without a finger occlusion problem. challenge: Direct-touch input enables intuitive operation and touch panels have been mounted on various devices. However, finger occlusion becomes one of the problems when a user touches the display from the front side. approach: I’ve proposed the emerging interactive transparent display named ‘‘LimpiDual Touch’’. Users can operate from the rear side of the display while seeing both displayed images and their fingers. This feature resolves a finger occlusion problem. Additionally, LimpiDual Touch also works for multiple users. Two users can share an identical display from both sides and control displayed objects at one time. This feature will be applicable to a collaborative work and an entertainment application. result: I’ve demonstrated this display and proved both-sided input had significant difference compared to front-side input as for the accuracy of touch input. In 2008, LimpiDual Touch was selected for ACM SIGGRAPH Student Research Competition and won the Japan Good Design Award. workcat: Academic Work pjtype: Scientific Research Oriented phase: Concept, Design, Develop, Evaluate period: 04/2007 - 03/2009 role: Researcher method: Technology Research, Prototyping device: Unique HW tech: Touch Interface, Non-Organic Transparent EL Display, OpenGL team: Business strategist, Infra technology specialist, Designer (Me) output: Research Paper, User Evaluation Sheet release: affiliatelink: http://www.engadget.com/2008/08/29/double-sided-transparent-touch-display-would-make-battleship-ama/
