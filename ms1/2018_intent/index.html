Intent for Final

Research | 2018, Academic Work

Context: what are you planning to research or investigate, and why (domain)? Who else is working in this area, and what are key creative precedents?

Network Science

The first domain I want to research is Network Science. When I reviewed my exploration so far, I found I am interested in creating a network in any kind of way. For example, in 2D and 4D, I tried to create a network and connect people based on background careers or question-answer conversation. Previously I said that my domain is social computing, however, social computing was still broad and it was more intriguing for me to see it from the perspective of the network.

Actually network science covers very broad area because in the current our world, we are surrounded by bunch of networks such as hypertext, social network and transportation network and so on.

Network science is an academic field which examines how network is created, expanded and operated. This field is close to mathematics, so I think that there might be areas that designers can intervene vice versa. So, there is little design work example but there is an interesting experiment. The small-world experiment comprised several experiments conducted by Stanley Milgram examining the average path length for social networks of people in the United States If you follow an average of six friends of friends, you can reach anyone in the united states.

Affective Interaction

3D-5D: Interaction through affective questions that can only be answered by people Affective design involves designing interfaces to enable human-computer interactions where emotional information is communicated by the user in a natural and comfortable way Affective computing is the study and development of systems and devices that can recognize, interpret, process, and simulate human affects. Fuehlometer (Feel-o-meter) is an interactive art installation that shows the mood of a city by displaying it in the form of a monumental Smiley. The system allows to read emotions out of random people’s faces. The faces are analyzed by sophisticated software (contributed by the Fraunhofer Institut). The obtained mood data are then stored on a server and processed by the smiley to visualize the emotions in real-time.
