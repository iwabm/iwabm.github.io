CompAge

2014, Pro Bono Consulting
Private Work
Designed and implemented a social competition service for visual designers using social APIs.

concept: Designers can show their talents and get work in a social platform. challenge: Many Japanese startups need impressive graphics for their businesses but almost all companies don’t have in-house designers. I wanted to support these companies and provide the field in which designers can show their talents. approach: Using CompAge, clients can hold graphic design competitions easily and cheaply. CompAge is consisted of following 3 key users. 1 - Clients who OFFER visual designs. 2 - Designers who REALIZE client’s images. 3 - Public users who EVALUATE submitted designs. A designer who selected by a client can get 80% of an award of a competition, and the rest 20% is distributed to top 5 designers who got a lot of “Like”. I’d like to provide the field which cultivates “Great design”. I’ve worked with friends and taken responsibility for the entire online service design and development from infrestructure to front-end. result: I’ve launched the service and held about 100 competitions in 3 months. Over 300 designers registered and over 500 visual designs were uploaded, but finally I couldn’t beat a similar big service and decided to pivot. workcat: Private Work pjtype: Service Design phase: Concept, Design, Build, Run period: 03/2011 - 07/2011 role: Design and Engineering Lead method: User-Centered Design, Waterfall engineering device: PC team: Sales, Designer, Engineer (Me) output: Service Running tech: Ruby on Rails, MySQL, html, css, jQuery release: 08/2011 - 11⁄2011
