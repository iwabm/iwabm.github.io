ChanoMa

2018 | Professional Project

Client: Chubu Electric Power(C-EP)

Role: Project Manager, UX/UI Design Lead

Contribution: Concept, Design, Build | 1 year

Outcome: iOS App (Planned to be released in March 2019)

Overview

ChanoMa (means “Living Room”) is a service for a dual-income households with a children whose age is under 9 years old to tighten family bonds.

Special features of this service is the following:

  • Play with children when parents are busy
    • because working mother’s life is terrible
  • Record and share children’s daily responses
    • because parents don’t understand children well

This is a client work for C-EP (Chubu Electric Power), which supplies electricity to 10 million households in Japan.

After the implementation. we performed a qualitative and quantitative user evaluation and 75% of participants evaluated “Very Positive” and “Positive” against the whole concept. Therefore, the client decide to launch this App in the next year cycle with considering qualitative feedback comments. Currently, the client continues to develop Android version and they are planning to release this product in 2019.

Design Process

Recently, a policy of electricity changed and the power grid began to be shared. A lot of competitors have been entering the electricity market and customer can choose company to look for better services. Thus C-EP must provide attractive services for users to improve its retention capability.

However, client is a traditional company and there is no designer in their company. So they are completely lost. To think about user, but how? This is the first design-driven project for them.

Therefore, I was employed as a project manager and a lead UX designer and managed an 10-person team consisting of designers, engineers and business strategists.

I involved in the project end to end from the concept, design and build using methodologies of Design Thinking, Lean Startup and Agile Development.

At the beginning, the first challenge was how to define user? The first thing I did was holding 2-days IBM Design Thinking workshop with clients.

There were 2 meanings. One is to explore possible users, and the other is to familiarize clients with a design process because clients were not familiar with collaborating with designers and design process.

In 2-days workshop, we defined 3 possible user segments according to social trends in Japan. We have more and more single, dual-income and elderly households, I divided all members including IBM designers and clients into 3 teams and discussed possible users and scenarios in the sticker and paper work.

In this project, we decided a dual-income working mother as a primary user from perspectives of both business and user.

According to our research, 49% of households are dual-income families in urban area in Japan and they will be a major segment of electricity user. And also they are actively searching for better and reasonable service, so we judged it is important to satisfy this user segment.

And in the following 10 interviews with working mother, we found how hard working mother’s life is. After coming back to the home, she must do cooking and many houseworks with taking care of children. And especially we found 2 core insights we should tackle.

One is the problem that recent mothers often passed their smartphones to a child when they were busy and showed YouTube and animation. It causes less communication between parents and children. Also, some young mothers even lose a way how to take care of children. They feel guilty to rely on a smartphone, but they can not quit it.

The other insight is mothers don’t know children’s feelings and preferences well. According to our interview, even mothers who are communicating with children usually say that they do not understand children’s feelings.

With iterative prototyping in collaboration with C-EP members, I came up with the idea that IoT toy plays and communicates with children when parents are busy. By recording children’s daily responses and sharing these with their parents, family members can understand their hidden feelings and preferences.

That was a interesting idea, However, Considering the project period and budget constraints, I decided to launch the App version as the first MVP. I regarded working mothers to rely on smartphones as a new habit and turn it into positive activities.

From that point, we did iterative creation with build/measure/learn framework.

The challenging part was user says unexpected comments and require functions which they personally want or there is not in the project scope. In that case, after the interview, we discussed the user comment together and prioritized in the one unified function list (namely product backlog).

Next Steps
Learnings

This is part of outcomes. I involved in the project end to end from concept, prototype and evaluation.

The biggest challenge in this project was some members such as client and engineer was not familiar with design process and collaboration with designer. Thus it is important to visualize designer’s mind and judge based on visualized content. In doing so, I could manage the project appropriately.
