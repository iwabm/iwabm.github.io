SoundPic

2010, iOS App
Private Work
Designed a prototype of camera App which records sounds and pictures simultaneously.

concept: People can record their memories vividly, share them quickly. challenge: To record our memories more vividly, we often use video. However, sometimes video isn’t convenient because file size is very large to record, save and share. approach: We came up with the idea to take a picture with audio. When a user release the shutter, he can take a picture with 4 sec sounds simultaneously. And he can share it easily because the size is small. In doing so, we can record and share memories easily and vividly. This was a comprehensive services distributed in the form of prototype camera device and iOS Camera App (no longer available) and the sharing platform on the web. This service also created brand-new photo experience such as users could view photos of “the same landscape, but completely different soundscape”. I worked for this project as a member of Gutygraph; prototyping geek unit. We were made up of 5 engineers specialized in UX, HW and Mobile UI. result: We prototyped the service comprehensively and exhibited in Make − Tokyo Meeting 06, Tokyo, Japan, 2010. workcat: Private Work pjtype: Service Design phase: Concept, Design, Build, Run period: 09/2010 - 11⁄2010 role: UI/UX Designer method: User-Centered Design, Waterfall engineering device: PC, iOS App, HW team: HW Specialist, UX Designer (Me), Engineers output: Prototype Release tech: Ruby on Rails, MySQL, Arduino
