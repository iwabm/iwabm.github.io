FUTURE SUPERMARKET DESIGN INAGEYA

Business Design | 2016, Professional Work

Objective

What should Inageya do to go “beyond” supermarket?

Inagea is a long-established supermarket in Japan. However, in a situation that society and family structures are changing, a role of supermarket also needs to change in Japan. Clients were struggling for planning a strong concept of a next-generation supermarket in 2020 with consideration for a sustainable ecosystem from producers to consumers and of course customer-first.

Approach

Co-create the concept using IBM Design Thinking

We gathered participants from all departments of the client and conducted a 2-day intensive workshop. We divided the members into three teams, and clutivated consumer-centered concepts from 3 different personas. The workshop includes following.

- Persona and Empathy Map
- As-Is User Scenario and Pain Points
- Ideation1 (Big Idea)
- Ideation2 (Future Speculation)
- Prioritization
- Storyboard
- Who/What/WOW
- Hills
- Feedback Grid
- Summarize Next Action

Result

Next-Generation Supermarket in 2020

Customers and their families can turn their household worries into fun and create their sustainable local communities together.

We finally reached integrated one concept of the next-gen supermarket and achieved a consensus beyond all departments of the client. In the last activity, we defined next To-Dos, and the client could go forward to a next step.

‘‘ I’ve never seen these sexy concepts come to life only in 2 days. ’’
- Executive of Inageya

‘‘ We need to get all our teams working like this one. ’’

My Role

Design Thinking Facilitator

As IBM Design Thinking instructor and facilitator, I planned and managed a discussion of entire workshop with collaborating with designers and retail-industry experts in IBM.

Design Process

2-day Intensive IBM Design Thinking Workshop

Using IBM Design Thinking, we supported to ideate a brand-new future user experience and defined the concept of next-gen supermarket only in 2 days intensive design camp. We started to define personas to understand our customers and then explored concrete ideas and abstract concepts; diverged and converged iteratively.

Understand

Persona and Empathy Map

In order to think of the supermarket in 2020, we defined 3 customer segments and personas who are highly likely to exist in Japan in the future.

As-Is Scenario Map and Defining Pain Points

We drew a scenario where each persona uses a supermarket and extracted 3 current pain points from each persona.

Explore

Big Idea & Future Speculation

We made an iterative ideation to solve the extracted pain points. We also thought of conceptual ideas that not only solve the most recent problems but also enhance a possibility of supermarket using imagery drawings of the future supermarket.

Prioritization

Each team took the feasibility on the horizontal axis and the importance on the vertical axis, prioritized the idea on two axes and finally selected three ideas.

Prototype

Storyboard

We visualized the idea as a series of experiences on the storyboard and prototyped a valuable experience to the customer in a higher resolution form.

Who/What/WOW

From drawn storyboard, We drafted the concept statement again in the Who-What-Wow format, and examined who is the user, what value it delivers, and whether it contains differentiated elements.

Hills (Visions ahead 1, 3, 5 years)

Finally we defined hills; the unique concept of the IBM Design Thinking. Hills is like the mission statements  for our next release and focus a project on big problems and outcomes for users, not just a list of feature requests. Therefore, it should be concrete, measurable and reasonable.

Evaluate

Feedback Grid and Define Next Action

Finally, through the workshop, we identified the next actions such as items with high risks and items to interview with the user, and the workshop was successfully completed!
