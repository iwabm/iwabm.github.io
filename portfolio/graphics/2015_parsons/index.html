PARSONS SUMMER

2015, Graphic Design | Academic Work

To be a versatile designer:

Learn World-Class Visual Communication Skills

To learn basic skills and philosophy of graphic design, I Took 3-weeks Advanced Graphic Design Summer Program in Parsons the new school of Design, US. During intensive 3-weeks project-based course, I delivered 6 projects; Poster Design, Bookjacket Design, Book Design, Timeline Design, App Design and Identity Design.

Poster Design

Using only type, made a poster which explains how to do something specific. I’ve dealt with “How to Ride NYC Subway“. I’ve used subway-like iconic circles and rectlinear text to conjure up subway in NYC.
Design Focus: Typography, Shape, Positive & Negative, B&W
Design Tool: Adobe Illustrator

Bookjacket Design

Designed a book jacket & matching slipcase reflecting the meaning of title of a novel. I’ve dealt with “Journey Without Maps“ by Graham Greene. I’ve imagined a long journey of letters and made a manual cut-and-paste design.
I’ve expressed the winding journey. The way is up and down. Time and weather always change; come and go. In some sense, it means a journey of our life.
Design Focus: Typography, Shape, Positive & Negative, Unity, B&W
Design Tool: Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator

Book Design

Designed 12-pages self-cover book based on Coney Island wandering. I’ve taken so many photos of Coney Island and dealt with materials I’ve found. Magenta rectangles are highlights to show each material.
Design Focus: Typography, Photography, Positive & Negative, Unity, 1-Color
Design Tool: Adobe Photoshop, Adobe InDesign

Timeline Design

Took the poster I’ve designed and added a time element using the Timeline Panel in Photoshop. I’ve designed the 70 sec movie which introduces steps to ride subway in NYC. Numbers in circles conjure up images of icons of subway in NYC.
Design Focus: Typography, Motion, Sound, Color
Design Tool:Adobe Photoshop

App Design

Designed App and Poster which introduce 5 spots of Coney Island. I’ve dealt with 5 flowers of Coney Island. This App introduces images and locations of flowers. I’ve imagined vivid colors of flowers as a ribbon and designed screens vertically.
Design Focus: Typography, Photography, Positive & Negative, Unity, 1-Color
Design Tool: Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Muse

Identity Design

Designed graphic identity for a festival event celebrating a neighborhood in NYC. I’ve dealt with Japanese Food Festival in East Village. I’ve used chopsticks as a symbol of this event and designed an envelope, invitation letter, bag, badge and small goods. To produce Japanese atomosphere, I’ve used Japanese papers and stereoscopic gimmicks like Ninja.
Design Focus: Typography, Unity, Texture, Material, Color
Design Tool: Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustator
